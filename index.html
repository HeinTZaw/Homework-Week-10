Sustainable Global Energy

Our everyday life depends on reliable and affordable energy. Yet energy is the contributing factor to climate change, accounting for 60 percent of total global greenhouse gas emissions.

The United Nations has established a goal of Ensuring universal access to affordable electricity by 2030, emphasising that to reach the goal requires investing in sustainable global energy.

The following data visualisation explores possible contributing factors of global energy and establishes a possible conclusion based on the factors and recommends a reasonable solution to allowing universal access to clean and affordable energy.

Contributing Factors of CO2 Emission

The following bubble plot explores if there is a link between GDP per capita and CO2 emission of a country. Each bubble represents a country and 6 different continents represent a variety of spreadness on the chart. Furthermore, a size channel is used to present relationships between the energy consumption per capita and CO2 emission.

It is apparent that China is the highest carbon dioxide producer despite low GDP per capita. There is not a huge relationship between GDP per capita and CO2 emissions as huge CO2 emitted countries still have low GDP per capita. By continent, European countries are wealthiest based on the scattered dataplot when compared and emitted more CO2 than other continents.

This is not surprising as developed countries have facilities that require a lot of energy and Europe has most of them .

In energy consumption, surprisingly, Europe andAsian countries tend to have huge consumption while African countries are low energy users. Click on the Year selection and choose from 2000 to 2019 to further explore the relationship.
